About Me

Hi, I'm Harshiv Gajjar — a Computer Science student at Toronto Metropolitan University with a strong foundation in programming, data analysis, and web development. I’m currently pursuing a minor in Mathematics and am passionate about blending technical knowledge with creativity.

Previously, I studied at Fergusson College in India, where I co-authored a published research paper on weather prediction using Raspberry Pi and the ARIMA model — combining hardware, data science, and real-world problem solving.

Beyond code, I’m a performing card magician (@harshivfools), and someone who’s deeply passionate about sports, animals, and learning new things. I’ve won awards for ethical hacking at IIT Bombay, and I currently work as a note taker at HabitatLearn, helping students access inclusive and high-quality academic content.

Whether I’m building a web app, analyzing algorithms, or captivating an audience with a deck of cards, I’m always driven by curiosity, empathy, and a desire to create meaningful experiences.

Let’s connect and make something unforgettable.
